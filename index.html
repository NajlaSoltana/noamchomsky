Dr. Noam Chomsky

The Father of Linguistics and a Computer Pioneer

Avram Noam Chomsky (born December 7, 1928) is an American linguist, philosopher, cognitive scientist, historian, political activist, and social critic. Sometimes described as "the father of modern linguistics", Chomsky is also a major figure in analytic philosophy and one of the founders of the field of cognitive science.

    Here's a time line of Dr. Chomsky's life:

  • 1928 born in Pennsylvania.
  • 1945–55 In 1945 Chomsky started studying at the University of Pennsylvania.
  • From 1951 to 1955 , at Harvard University, Chomsky undertook research on what would become his doctoral dissertation.
  • 1955–66 early career ranging between assistant professor,teaching a course on linguistics and philosophy, an influential scientist, a visiting professor.
  • 1967–75 Anti-Vietnam War activism and rise to prominence.
  • 1970s and 1980s, expansion of chomsky s publication.
  • 1980–89 Chomsky had established himself as a globally recognized figure.
  • 1990–present Chomsky embraced political activism to a greater degree than before.
